Why Dubai

The features every buyer asks about first

These are the six things that come up in almost every conversation with a UK or NRI buyer — so we lead with them, honestly, including the caveats.

01 / Tax

No personal property tax

Dubai levies no annual property tax, capital gains tax, or tax on rental income at the personal level. UK and Indian tax rules on your worldwide income may still apply to you personally — this is a UAE-side benefit, not a substitute for advice on your own tax position.

02 / Visa

A path to UAE residency

Property investment above the qualifying threshold can support a long-term UAE Golden Visa, giving you and eligible family members renewable residency — useful for buyers who want a base in the Gulf, not just a rental asset.

03 / Yield

Rental yields UK buyers notice

Gross yields in established Dubai communities have historically run well above typical UK buy-to-let returns. Figures vary by building, area and market cycle — treat any number you're given as indicative, not promised.

04 / Ownership

Full freehold, as a foreigner

In designated freehold areas, foreign buyers — resident or not — can own property outright, not on leasehold, with title registered in their own name via the Dubai Land Department.

05 / Safety

Regulated transactions

Off-plan payments in Dubai are legally required to sit in RERA-supervised escrow accounts, released to the developer against construction milestones — a structural protection buyers specifically ask about.

06 / Lifestyle

Connectivity & livability

Direct flights from most UK cities, a large resident South Asian and British community, and infrastructure built around global residents — relevant if this is a future home, not only an investment.

The questions only London & NRI buyers ask

REMOTE

Can I really buy without flying out?

Yes — KYC, reservation, and signing can be completed remotely with your matched broker, with a site visit optional rather than required before completion.

FX

How do I handle GBP → AED currency risk?

Your broker can talk you through staged payment plans and currency timing options — this is a decision worth taking independent FX advice on, especially for larger sums.

REPATRIATION

Can I get rental income and sale proceeds back to the UK or India?

The UAE has no capital controls on repatriation, but your own tax residency, and any UK or India-UAE tax treaty position, will determine what you owe at home. We'll flag this — we won't advise on it ourselves.

FINANCE

Are Sharia-compliant or NRI-specific mortgages available?

A number of UAE banks and financiers offer Islamic and non-resident financing structures. Your matched broker can point you to lenders who work with UK/NRI applicants.

AFTERCARE

Who manages the property once I own it?

Most buyers on this route don't relocate immediately. Property management, tenanting and maintenance are typically arranged through the broker's partner network after handover.

Before you ask

Frequently asked, answered plainly

Do I pay anything to use this service?

No. There is no fee to buyers at any stage. Our introduction fee is paid by the Dubai broker or developer once a transaction completes — it's built into how the industry already operates, not added to your price.

Is this you selling me property directly?

No. We're an independent introducer. We take your requirement and pass it to one regulated Dubai brokerage partner who runs the actual sales process, viewings, contracts and after-sales support.

Can I buy Dubai property without visiting the UAE?

In most cases, yes — reservation, KYC, and signing can be completed remotely, with a visit optional rather than mandatory. Your matched broker will confirm what applies to the specific property.

What's the minimum investment for a Golden Visa?

As a general guide, UAE property-based Golden Visas have been available from AED 2 million (roughly £425,000–£450,000 depending on exchange rates) in qualifying property. Thresholds and rules are set by UAE authorities and can change — your broker will confirm current criteria before you commit to anything.

Is my money protected during construction (off-plan)?

Regulated developers are required to hold buyer payments in RERA-supervised escrow accounts, releasing funds against verified construction milestones rather than upfront. Always confirm a project's RERA registration and escrow status before paying anything.

What about UK or Indian tax on my Dubai property?

The UAE doesn't tax personal rental income or capital gains, but your UK tax residency (and, for NRIs, your Indian tax position) can still create obligations on income or gains at home. This is genuinely worth a conversation with a qualified UK/India tax adviser before you buy — we don't provide tax or financial advice ourselves.

Who exactly sees my details?

Only the single Dubai broker we match you with, based on your stated budget and goals. We don't sell or forward your details to multiple developers or call centres.
